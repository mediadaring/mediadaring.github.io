To start this, keep in mind that 1 litres is the same as 1 [tex]dm^3[/tex].

So, it will be better if we transform every known variables into dm.

With 1 cm = 0.1 dm,

80 cm = 80(0.1) = 8 dm

90 cm = 90(0.1) = 9 dm

Water half of its height means that the container is half full.

Since the water inside the container is 360 litres (that is, half of the container), the full volume of the container will be 360 / (1/2) = 720 litres.

720 litres = 720 [tex]dm^3[/tex]

Now, let's use the volume formula of cuboids to find the container's height.

Container's volume = Base dimension x Height

720 = 8 x 9 x Height

Height = 720/72

Height = 10 dm

Use the conversion rate 1 dm = 10 cm,

height = 10(10) = 100 cm

The height of the container is 100 cm.
